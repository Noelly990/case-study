My role as a UX designer was to conduct research into the clients previous website and assess what needed to be removed or updated, prior to the kick-off. Then, I worked extensively on user research and user flows in order to cater the site to its primary users.
The main challenge during the first phase of the redesign was deciding what direction the website should go in since I wasnt contracted by the client. Another issue I faced was the previous design did not take into consideration the stores full inventory. I also wantedto add multiple features including the ability to buy online which was not an option before.
I created a user flow of the primary users of the website. Once I had a solid understanding of who the primary users of the site were going tobe, I was able to come up with design ideas for theredesign. I took into consideration the color scheme, the new features, and any other concerns that came along the way.

With Covid-19 being a major disruptor in store fronts, I focused on the shift Tops Art Supplies would need to make in order to serve as a more prominent competitor with the big chains. This meant, the primary user of the site would be an arts student. But through research, I also learned the new site needed to be a source for prospective users to find inspiration for new crafts. With Covid-19 theres been a 61% increase in arts and crafts in the united states.As a secondary user, I created a user that would be locating the site due to these special circumstances.
Lydia is an art student at UCLA. Shes always looking for stores close to her that have high quality products.
Dan works as a Software engineer but due to Covid, he currently works from home. His kids also are being schooled from home so he is able to spend a lot more time with them. Dan is looking for crafts to do with his children to bond with them and teach them to be creative.
